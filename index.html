Introduction

The COVID-19 pandemic has significantly impacted all of us in different ways. With cases ramping up daily in Quebec, the government has established some restrictions in order to reduce them. Human interactions between citizens have been reduced to only their own households. Furthermore, with the advancement of technology, the human lifespan has been expanded and it has given the elderly methods to engage themselves with others. Although the newest technology seems to have high learning curves, elderly people still try to learn these new technologies. According to a recent study conducted by Pew Research Center Found that among seniors ages 65 and above, about 85% owned a cell phone. Although they do not know how to use all the functionalities that they offer, they can use the basic applications to call others and entertain themselves. 

The problem

Knowing that the maintenance of well-being, healthcare, and social connection is crucial for the elderly, these restrictions have dramatically affected the elderly population. In nursing homes and assisted-living facilities across the province, visitors are not allowed, which may affect the mental health of some residents. With the interdiction of visits in these facilities, the interaction between the residents and the outside is limited and almost reduced to none. With nothing to distract themselves, elders have to entertain themselves with smartphones and televisions but these activities can become unsatisfying if done repetitively. Therefore, the human interaction that they seek the most is missing in their daily lives. 

Solution

Our team has come up with a mobile application that would possibly help the elderly maintain their well-being and mental healthcare. This software will allow elders to connect with their peers via video call and chat while respecting social distancing. Unlike regular chatting applications, ours will only allow a specific type of user to limit the number of pranksters. Therefore, this application can only be accessed by elders and volunteers who have to complete some volunteering hours for their program. Additionally, our application will allow elders to connect with new people if their current entourage is busy. Through a matching system, elders will be able to chat with other elderly, young volunteers who wish to help society. 

Research

Understanding one's user base is key for a UX designer to design both an appealing, but also easy-to-use application. Consequently, in order to gather data but also respect COVID-19 restrictions, we opted to ask our entourage as well as their grandparents for their thoughts and opinions on the mental health matter through Google Forms.

 

Analyzing the data confirmed our initial theories that mental health has been declining since the start of the pandemic, given the lack of social activities and interactions with others. Personas

After analyzing our research results, we created three fictitious personas with different ages, careers, and backgrounds. This was done for us to better understand our user base, their needs, and their preferences.

Figure 1: Gertrude's Persona

Gertrude Antoinette is an elderly at Floralies Lachine retirement home who was a high-level executive at Walmart. Today, in retirement, she enjoys discussion with nurses and used to be a Bingo fanatic before the pandemic. But with the pandemic, nurses have received a huge workload which reduces the interaction between her and her helpers. Now, she spends most of her time facing the outside through the window wishing that there would be a youngster who would listen to her stories. 


Figure 2: Gary's Persona

Garry Smith loves to give. As a nursing home worker, he dedicates his life to making the world a better place. However, not every single resident can be cared for by him. Through the usage of DEEZCORD, more residents will be able to have meaningful conversations with others. Garry is glad to help residents through their technical needs, especially as many of them are not used to technological devices and their quirks.


Figure 3: Stefanie's Persona

Stefanie Li is a medical student who enjoys helping her neighborhood. During her studies at Marianopolis college, she was actively doing volunteering work to support local businesses. Sadly, due to the pandemic, she was unable to help out due to Covid-19 restrictions. With DEEZCORD, she is able to help back in a different way and will be able to connect with the elderly. Furthermore, these volunteering hours will be recorded in her academic performance which will help her get into a well known medical university. 

User Goals

Both expectations and goals of our users became clearer after creating and analyzing our personas. The primary goal is without a doubt to have meaningful social interactions with others during the COVID-19 Pandemic. These meetings may blossom into permanent friendships and planned activities together afterwards. A secondary, underlying goal would be to help others while helping ourselves, especially in these times where we are extremely disconnected and divided from one another. 

 

User Journey

We decided to map our different users' journeys through our application in order to help us understand their flow and decision-making. As a result, underlying pain points with our application were discovered and fixed in order to make one's experience as smooth and easy as possible.

Figure 4: Gertrude's User Journey

 

As a technology notice, Gertrude found it complicated and annoying to fill in all the information after signing up. However, that pain point was soon erased as she met someone enjoyable and friendly. She was very happy to have made another friend. She found the font and layout very easy to use, especially for someone who doesn't use her phone much. 


Figure 5: Gary's User Journey

Gary did not enjoy the long forms to fill for his account. He prefers an application that takes less information about the user because he thinks the computer is collecting data from him. Other than that, he liked the application because it is easy to use and reminds him of other connecting applications. Overall, the experience was fun because he was able to chat with someone funny. 


Figure 6: Stephanie's User Journey

Stephanie likes the application overall, but she was scared of getting cat-fished, meaning that the profile of one user could correspond to another one. She enjoyed the experience because of the video call which creates the meaningful human interactions that are missing during this pandemic. Furthermore, the application has a nice design, and she would use it again to connect with other elders. 

Color Palette

 

Figure 7: Project color palette

 

Both bright and vibrant colors were an obvious good choice for our application. This is because of our focus on helping mental health through social interactions and friendship forming. Not only do these fun colors help accentuate the light and relaxed mood of our application, but they also help ease the user in with a sense of comfort.

Typography

Similarly to the color palette, we decided to choose typography that will allow elderly people to easily read and surf through the application with comfort. Therefore, we chose 2 fonts to use in our application. 

 

The "Roboto Mono" font was chosen as the main font as its simplicity and modernism makes it both easy to read and visually aesthetic. Furthermore, the font size selected will be at least 20 px to allow elders and the visually impaired to read comfortably. By avoiding small fonts, we try to make the user experience better for our target users.

Wireframes and User Flow

The wireframe is the skeleton of the application which allowed us to create a detailed prototype of our project. At this stage, the user interface and style were applied in general and we show in the interaction between the user and the platform. Using the mockups, we made an actual user interface showing all interactions between the different screens. We started making each page based on the sketches and linked them together depending on which button would be clicked by the user. These are the basic interactions that our personas would perform.  

Figure 10: UI Prototype of the application

Final thoughts

In conclusion, as our application is mainly targeted towards the elderly, we made sure to keep the user interface simple and intuitive to use, with a minimal number of pages to increase learnability and memorability. Through research and user personas/journeys, we came to understand our user base and their different pain points. We designed our application with their perspectives in mind. As a result, we finished with a both relaxed and fun design, giving off a fun aura for our users. It was extremely enjoyable for us to come up with this application, as none of our team members had any past experience with user interface design. Therefore, we definitely gained a lot of knowledge in understanding the customer and its point of view, all with the goal of designing a modern application accessible to all.
